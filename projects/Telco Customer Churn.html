Project Overview

This project analyzed customer churn for a telecommunications company using descriptive statistics and visualizations. It focused on demographic, financial, and service-related factors to identify drivers of attrition, providing actionable insights to reduce customer turnover. Key visualizations highlighted critical trends for strategic decision-making.

A total of 26.5% of customers churned, with senior citizens disproportionately likely to leave. Gender showed no significant impact, while variability in total charges correlated with churn. Service features like contract duration and internet type were flagged for further analysis.

DMissing "TotalCharges" values were converted to zero for analysis, and "SeniorCitizen" data was recoded categorically (Yes/No) for clarity. These steps ensured reliable statistical outcomes and streamlined trend visualization.

Retention efforts should prioritize senior citizens and address billing inconsistencies. Further segmentation by contract types and service features is recommended to refine strategies and enhance customer engagement.
